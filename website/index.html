Zoom's Calendar Redesign

A UX Case Study by David Thai.

  • Contact me

Summary

Zoom links are hard to use. There's no great Zoom built-in function to help you manage or organize them. I built a calendar tab for Zoom's interface to allow for a time-based and intutive way for users to interact with their online schedules.

Why Redesign?

My inspiration came from looking at Spotify's UI. I felt that it should be as easy to see your upcoming and recently visited meetings as finding your liked songs on Spotify. Here were some problems I discovered during User Testing:

  • Difficult to store / organize Zoom links
  • Incompatibility between Calendar apps (lack of native calendar in Zoom)
  • Confusion in how meeting links are used

Figure 1: Spotify's interface which lets you access recently visited songs.

Project Goals

1. Build Zoom Calendar Tab

The lack of a native calendar for scheduling and viewing upcoming meetings in the Zoom app forces users to use third party calendars, such as Google and Microsoft Calendars. For users who do not already use Google and Microsoft, this is an inconvenience because they are forced to choose a third party calendar that is not already available with Zoom. Users also may want to separate their Zoom calendars from their Google/Microsoft calendars, but are left with no choice but to create a separate account to use their Microsoft/Google integrated calendars separately from their non-Zoom integrated calendars. By having Zoom provide a native calendar, users can easily plan their meetings without having to use third party calendars. The option to integrate third party calendars should still be available, but not forced upon the users. A native calendar can also be a great feature to store, organize, and access their Zoom link meetings and provide a solution to the initial issue mentioned earlier.

Data Science

The target audience is anyone who uses some form of video conferencing software regularly, which includes any profession with reduced in-person communication due to the pandemic. To better understand how users feel about the current Zoom interface and our proposed improvements, I conducted surveys and interviews to collect data.

The ideal sample size would be at least thirty survey respondents. This will greatly aid in my ability to conduct a statistical analysis and produce results I am confident in. Qualitative data will provide me with more open ended answers, opinions, and insights to better help us recognize trends and commonalities among users. I plan to use the master apperintence model, whereby interviewees explain their observations to validate the reasoning behind my proposed solutions and allow me to form a tenable hypothesis. Surveys, although not as detailed, will help extract quantitative data from a larger sample size in a shorter time frame. On the other hand, interviews are more time consuming, which restricts the sample size, but I will be able to collect high quality data since I can communicate with the users directly and ask open-ended questions.

Research Findings

About 83% of the sample users use Zoom regularly every week (3-20 hours). In addition, 100% of the people who took the survey were college students. I would have preferred to reach a larger demographic age-wise, but due to time limitations, I was only able to gather data amongst fellow students. For calendars used, there was a wide variety of answers. Surprisingly, some people didn’t even use a calendar, and many others used varying calendars such as Canvas calendar, Apple calendar, Google calendar, and Microsoft Outlook. In terms of the users’ experience using external calendars, the results were a bit ambiguous. The mean was roughly a 3 from a likert scale of 1-5; users were neither extremely happy nor dissatisfied with their interfaces. Moreover, 75% of users voted in favor of using a native Zoom calendar if it existed. Additionally, 10/18 of our users did not use calendars. The remaining 8 of them did, and their average rating of the Zoom Calendar experience was a 2.875/5, which was higher than I expected.
